Summary – Renewable Energy

Renewable energy sources such as solar and wind are rapidly replacing fossil-fuel generation around the world. Technological improvements and falling costs have transformed renewables into the most cost-effective new sources of electricity in many regions. Large-scale investments in infrastructure, battery storage, and grid modernization now enable better integration of variable energy sources into national grids. Solar capacity has grown exponentially due to affordable panels and improved efficiency. Wind energy, both onshore and offshore, has expanded with more powerful turbines capturing energy at greater heights. Together, these sources significantly reduce carbon emissions, improve air quality, and lessen dependence on fossil fuels. Governments and private sectors are increasingly focused on achieving net-zero targets by expanding renewable capacity. Continued research into hydrogen, battery innovation, and smart-grid technologies will accelerate the global transition toward clean, sustainable energy.

Reference: https://www.iea.org/reports/renewables-2023

Technical Description

This application uses Angular 21 as a fully standalone SPA with route guards and JWT-based authentication handled through a FastAPI backend. The backend provides hardcoded test credentials and dynamic REST endpoints serving JSON chart data. An HTTP interceptor attaches JWT tokens automatically to requests. The frontend uses Chart.js via Ng2-Charts to visualize asynchronous data. Angular’s standalone architecture minimizes boilerplate and improves modularity.
